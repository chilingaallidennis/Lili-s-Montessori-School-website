About Us

Why Lili's Montessori School?

Lili’s Montessori School – LMNS was established out of a need to provide a real Montessori school setting in Lilongwe and the Community in and around the Suburb of Area 10.We have Preschool and Lower Elementary. We enroll children from 2.5 years to 6 years old in preschool and from 6 years up to 12 years in Low Elementary and have a capacity for 70 children. We have Four teaching staff on our Team at Preschool that are qualified to teach the 3-6 Montessori environment. Ms Martha and Ms Sarah hold AMI Diplomas from the Montessori Training Centre at Shingle Springs, California. They have 10 years teaching experience between them, Ms Hellen and Ms Ritah are our Assistant Teacher’s currently studying towards their 3-6 Diploma. In Low elementary, we have four teaching staff, Ms Cindy Costa, Mr Dennis Chilinga who is a graduate from the university of Malawi, formerly known as chancellor college and currently going through his montessori diploma of 6-9, Mr Elias who has a Montessori Diploma from the Montessori Training Centre at Shingle Springs, California. We maintain a 1:12 ratio teacher/ child ratio ensuring optimum attention and develop an intimate relationship between the teachers and the children under our care.

Montessori Program

Our Program is based on the preschool type of introduction to the many Montessori School concepts. Classrooms are for mixed ages, allowing for both individual and social development. The learning materials are openly displayed on low shelves for easy access and the environment enables the children to become proactive participants in their learning.We draw our teachings and inspiration from the philosophy of Dr Maria Montessori and with the help of the specialized Montessori materials, the different cultures from around the world as well as outdoors activities. We have developed a curriculum that is broad-based and interdisciplinary.We believe the children and adults i.e. teachers and parents are collaborators in the educational process which leads the child move or develop from concrete experiences and concepts to abstract understandings for future academic success.
